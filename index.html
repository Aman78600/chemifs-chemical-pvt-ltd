Some Industries We Serve!

Water Treatment

The standard process for potable water treatment involves clean water from a lake, reservoir or river being transferred into a tank where coagulation, flocculation and clarification occurs by the addition of Poly Aluminium Chloride. This is followed by sedimentation, where the heavy particles (floc) settle to the bottom, and the clear water moves to filtration.

Pulp and Paper Industry

The main features of PAC are its high Al₂O₃ content and polymeric nature, which give rise to products of hydrolysis characterized by a high molecular weight and a large number of positive electric charges able to interact with the cellulose fibers and size, optimizing the sizing phase and the retention of fibers.

Cosmetics & Personal Care

Aluminium Chlorohydrate is widely used in the cosmetics and personal care industry, primarily as an active ingredient in deodorants and antiperspirants. Known for its efficiency in reducing perspiration and controlling odor, along with proven safety and stability, it remains a preferred choice for leading global brands in personal care formulations.

Product Applications

Application in Wastewater Treatment

PAC are used in any existing water Treatment plant. It requires less alkali addition and has greater flock formation, leading to more rapid settling and longer filter runs. The feeding rate of doses should be changed accordingly to the quality of raw water and it is necessary to determine an optimum feeding rate beforehand by means of Jar test.

Key Benefits:

  • The ability to function at lower dosages, leading to lower sludge volumes
  • The metal content is negligible
  • Very low residual aluminium concentration in treated water

Applications:

  • Municipal & Industrial potable water treatments
  • Industrial wastewater treatments
  • Sewage water treatment
  • Consumption of PAC quantity is very low compared to alum, reducing costs

Sizing Agent in Paper Making

Paper Industries use PAC as a rosin sizing control in Neutral and alkaline paper production that affects the drainage of liquor from the paper. It provides many advantages for paper and pulp manufacturers as it provides good results and dosage levels are low compared to alum.

Benefits:

  • Improves paper quality
  • Improves filler and fines retention
  • Improves machine and drainage speed
  • Reduces the discharged load to the wastewater treatment plant
  • Polymers for retention and save-all operations

Additional Advantages:

  • Reduction in the use of other wet end additives such as size chemical, retention polymers and starch
  • Less colour reversion in paper sheet
  • Sulphate free environment
  • Reduces brittleness of paper sheets

Other Industries

We cater to various industries including textiles for dyeing processes, glassmaking for purity enhancement, pharmaceuticals for chemical synthesis, and food industries for processing and preservation.
